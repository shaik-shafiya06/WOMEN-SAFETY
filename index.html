About Women Safety Platform

Women Safety is a dedicated platform committed to providing comprehensive safety resources, emergency support, and empowerment tools for women across India. We connect you with government helplines, legal resources, and community support.

Our mission is to ensure every woman has access to immediate help, legal guidance, and support services during times of need. We provide 24/7 emergency assistance, location tracking for safety, and direct access to all government helplines.

Through our platform, we work towards creating a safer environment for women with real-time emergency response, awareness programs, and comprehensive support services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about filing complaints and using our services

You can file a complaint online by filling out the complaint form below, sending an email to complaints@ncw.nic.in, calling our helpline at 7827-170-170, or visiting our office in person. Online complaints are processed faster and you receive an instant complaint ID.

You need to provide: your full name, contact details (phone and email), state/location, type of complaint, and a detailed description of the incident including dates, places, and persons involved. Supporting documents can be uploaded if available.

Yes, absolutely. All complaints are handled with strict confidentiality. Your personal information is protected and only shared with authorized personnel working on your case. We follow strict privacy protocols to ensure your safety.

Initial acknowledgment is sent within 48 hours. The complete investigation and resolution time varies depending on the complexity of the case, typically ranging from 2 weeks to 3 months. You can track your complaint status anytime using your complaint ID.

Yes, you can file a complaint on behalf of another woman if she is unable to do so herself. Please provide details of both the complainant and the affected person, along with the reason why she cannot file directly.

NCW handles complaints related to: domestic violence, workplace harassment, dowry harassment, sexual assault, discrimination, cyber crimes against women, denial of rights, property disputes, and other issues affecting women's safety and dignity.

In case of immediate danger, call: National Women Helpline 181, Police Women Helpline 1091, Emergency Police 100, or NCW Helpline 7827-170-170. These helplines are available 24√ó7. You can also visit the nearest police station immediately.

No, you don't need a lawyer to file a complaint with NCW. Our team provides free legal guidance and support. However, if you need legal representation in court, we can connect you with our legal aid services and panel of lawyers.
